About TransNav

An Overview

Started by the Vreeken family in 1981 out of a bedroom, today TransNav has production facilities across the United States and Mexico, as well as strategic partnerships around the globe.

TransNav is a family owned and operated plastic manufacturing and trading company. Founded by Dutch immigrants, TransNav has steadily grown to become one of the most reputable manufacturing companies in multiple industries.

Through years of experience in manufacturing, our team has developed relationships around the world to satisfy our customers manufacturing needs.

A Brief History

1980s

TransNav began in 1981 as a subsidiary of Montreal based FedNav.

The Vreeken family purchased TransNav in 1983 and began operations in St. Clair Shores, MI. Partnerships formed with several Portugese mold-makers in 1984 to design tools and build injection molds for the US market.

In 1986 TransNav formed several new companies to handle various aspects of the growing business, at this time operations were moved to Mt. Clemens to accommodate this growth.

1990s

During the 1990s TransNav outgrew its facilities twice. The first time in 1991 when they began molding operations in Clinton Twp, and the second time in 1995 when the company moved to New Baltimore.

In 1998, in an effort to better serve Volkswagen de Mexico, the Vreeken family formed TransNav Mexico in Cuautalancingo, Puebla. While taking on operations in Mexico, the commercial and operations teams in the United States welcomed the addition of a design team.

TransNav capped off the decade by forming a partnership with Hornschuch A.G. of Germany, to provide leatherette to the North American automotive market.

2000s

In the early part of the 2000s both TransNav Technologies, and TransNav Mexico outgrew their facilities and moved operations to meet production volume demands of customers.

TransNav added another partnership in 2005, this time with Hong Kong based Hayco Ltd.

In 2008, TransNav outgrew existing facilities and purchased its current facilities at Cricklewood Blvd. This move gave way for more large capacity presses and tool making. TransNav Mexico acquired a 3300 ton press to complement the existing 4400 ton press and completed building expansion in the same year.

2010s

In 2011, TrasnNav expanded the Cricklewood facilities in an effort to meet demands for increased production volume, without moving operations.

In 2012, TransNav acquired Perfection Components in Danville, KY.

In June, 2014 TransNav purchased the of assets of Plasti-cart and formed a new company Trans-Cart, LLC to produce hand baskets and shopping carts for the retail industry.

In early 2015, TransNav will take delivery of a 4000 ton press for its Michigan facility. Also planned for 2015, TransNav will double the size of its tool room, secondary/automation, research and development center. The new space will house molding machines up to 360 tons.
